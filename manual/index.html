What is Verdant

Verdant is a slow, multiplayer, browser-based game. You control a Pilgrim who wanders a world of fifteen named locations connected by walking paths.

Your pilgrim exists on a shared server between sessions. While you are away, time still passes — plants grow, fellow pilgrims wander.

There is no winning condition. The goal is to fulfil your personal Vision, explore the world, leave your mark in gardens, and encounter other pilgrims along the way.

Getting Started

Open the game in a browser and press Become a Pilgrim. Your pilgrim is created at a random location, carrying the origin seed of that location.

You are assigned a Vision — four rules about the state of the world's gardens. These guide what you plant and where you travel.

Your pilgrim is tied to the device you use. Do not clear your browser's local storage or you will lose access to your pilgrim.

Travelling

From the Location tab, the Travel section shows paths you can walk. Each path shows how long it will take. Tap Walk to begin.

Before walking, you will be asked which seed to carry on the journey. Choose from the seeds available at your current location.

On the path

While walking, a path diagram shows your progress. The ↩ Reverse button sends you back the way you came. Enable Auto-arrive to skip the arrival screen and land directly at your destination.

You can also change which seed you are carrying while in transit.

The map

Switch to the Map tab to see the world. Locations you have visited are shown with their origin seed icon. Tap a visited location, then press Travel to X to queue a multi-hop route — your pilgrim will walk each leg in sequence.

Arrival

When you arrive, you see the Arrival screen. This lists pilgrims you encountered on the journey and lets you choose your carried seed. Press Continue to enter the location.

Seeds & Pots

Each location has a fixed number of pots. Pots can be planted with seeds and grow through five stages:

StageAppears after
SeedJust planted
Seedling30 minutes
Grown6 hours
Fruiting7 days
Dead30 days

Planting

In the Nursery widget, select a seed, then tap a pot to plant it. Planting costs 1 energy and puts you into a tending state for a short time (longer for older plants you are replacing).

The nursery shows the origin seed of the location, your carried seed, seeds from grown or fruiting pots here, and seeds carried by other resting pilgrims.

Clearing

With None selected in the nursery, tap a pot to clear it. The pot settles for a period before it can be replanted.

Decorating

You can Decorate a pot you did not plant. Decorations mark your appreciation and count toward the pot's score in your Record.

Carrying a seed

You carry one seed at a time. Your carried seed contributes to the nursery pool at locations you visit, allowing other pilgrims to plant it.

Vision

Your Vision is four rules assigned when you become a pilgrim. Each rule describes a condition that must be true at a number of locations you have visited.

Example rules:

  • "3 locations where at least one pot is fruiting"
  • "4 locations with pots of two different seed types"
  • "6 locations where a pot has been decorated"

You can see your Vision progress on the Location tab (Vision section) and on the Record tab. The Location tab shows whether your current location satisfies each rule.

Completing a rule

When a rule is satisfied at enough locations, it is marked Complete. Completing a rule gives you a permanent +10% movement speed bonus and increases your maximum energy by 1.

Dismissing a rule

On the Record tab you can Dismiss an incomplete rule. The slot is replaced with a new rule after a delay. Use this if a rule no longer fits your playstyle.

Energy

You start with 3 energy. Energy is spent planting seeds. Milestones that increase your maximum energy:

  • +1 after your pilgrim is 1 day old
  • +1 after your pilgrim is 1 week old
  • +1 if you have visited all 15 locations
  • +1 per completed Vision rule

Energy does not regenerate mid-play — it is restored fully when you return from a walk (between sessions the server does not regenerate it automatically; the current version ties energy to these milestones).

Encounters

As you walk a path you may encounter other pilgrims travelling the same path in the opposite direction. Encounters are shown as a list while walking and again on the Arrival screen.

You can copy a seed from an encountered pilgrim. This does not remove their seed — it is a copy. You can only carry one seed at a time, so copying replaces whatever you were carrying.

The Record

Switch to the Record tab to see your pilgrim's history:

  • Age — how long your pilgrim has existed
  • Energy — current and maximum, with milestone progress
  • Vision — all four rules and their progress
  • Movement speed — your current speed bonus from completed rules
  • Seed Log — which seeds you have carried, seen as seedlings, grown, fruiting, and dead

Deleting your pilgrim

At the bottom of the Record tab is a Delete my Pilgrim button. This permanently removes your pilgrim and all their decorations from the server. This cannot be undone.

Running a Server

⚠ Security warning — please read before hosting

The Verdant server is designed as a simple personal/community toy. It has:

  • No authentication — anyone who knows your URL can join and play
  • No rate limiting — a client can send actions as fast as it wants
  • No input sanitisation beyond basic checks — untrusted inputs reach the game logic directly
  • World state stored in a single JSON file — no backups, no transactions
  • No HTTPS by default — traffic is unencrypted unless you add a reverse proxy

Do not expose this server to the public internet without understanding these risks. It is suitable for a small group of trusted friends on a private network or behind a VPN, or as a learning exercise.

Requirements

  • Node.js 18 or later
  • npm

Setup

# Clone or download the repository
git clone https://github.com/your-username/pilgrim.git
cd pilgrim

# Install dependencies
npm install

# Start the server
npm start

The server runs on port 3000 by default. Visit http://localhost:3000 in a browser to play.

Configuration

Set the PORT environment variable to change the port:

PORT=8080 npm start

Persistent state

World state is saved to server/data/state.json on every action. The file is created automatically. Back it up regularly if you care about your world.

If the server detects a state file from an unsupported version it will migrate the data or start fresh.

Deploying to Fly.io

The repository includes a fly.toml configuration. If you have the flyctl CLI installed and an account:

fly launch   # first time
fly deploy   # subsequent deploys

HTTPS / reverse proxy

To add HTTPS, place Nginx or Caddy in front of the Node.js process and proxy WebSocket connections. WebSockets require the Upgrade header to be forwarded.

Example Caddy configuration:

example.com {
  reverse_proxy localhost:3000
}
